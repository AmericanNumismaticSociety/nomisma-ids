      <pre>1826 Khisht Tepe on the Oxus River, 
    province of Qunduz, c. 110 km. E of Balkh 
    (anc. Bactra, Bactriana), 1946
    Burial: after 140 B.C. (Fussman); c. 100 
        B.C. (Bivar)
    Contents: 627+ AR
    Alexander III: 1 plated tetradr. (unc. mint) 
    Antiochus II: 4 tetradr. (<span rel="mint" resource="bactra">Bactra with por-
        trait of Diodotus</span>)
    Antiochus Hierax: 1 tetradr. (<span rel="mint" resource="ilium">Ilium</span>) 
    Alexander I Bala: 1 tetradr. (<span rel="mint" resource="seleuceia_ad_tigrim">Seleuceia ad 
        Tigrim</span>)
    Diodotus, <span rel="mint" resource="bactria">Bactria: 1 tetradr.</span>
    Euthydemus I: 12 tetradr.
    Demetrius I: 8 tetradr. 
    Euthydemus II: 5 tetradr. 
    Agathocles: 3 tetradr. 
    Antimachus Theos: 14 tetradr.
    Demetrius II: 50 tetradr.
    Eucratides I: 147 tetradr. (3 with rev. 
        Heliocles and Laodice)
    Eucratides I or II: 130 tetradr. (rev. Apollo) 
    Plato: 12 tetradr.
    Heliocles I: 204 tetradr.; 17 dr.
    Lysias: 4 tetradr.
    Theophilus: 1 tetradr. 
    Antialcidas: 3 tetradr. 
    Amyntas: 5 double-decadr. 
    Archebius: 2 tetradr. 
    Philoxenus: 1 tetradr. 
    Hermaeus: 1 tetradr.
    Disposition: Cabul 627
    Bivar, NNM India 3 (1955), illus. (= JNSI 1955,
        pp. 37-52)
    Curiel-Fussman, Le trésor monétaire de Qunduz 
        (Paris, 1965), illus.
    Several coins appearing in commerce during 
        the 1950's and 1960's seem to derive 
        from this hd., which may have been 
        considerably larger than originally 
        assumed. The distinctions between 
        homonymous kings in the list above 
        are disputed in some cases. Especially 
        the existence of two king Eucratides 
        may be doubted
