725 Thrace?, 1920's
    Burial: c. 340-335 B.C. (T)
    Contents: 84+ AR
    <span rel="mint" resource="byzantium">Byzantium: 61 dr.; 21 triob.; 2 diob.</span> 
    Disposition: Istanbul
    N. Olcay reports the acquisition of 84 coins 
        of Byzantium from a hd. found in 
        Thrace or Asia Minor. This is very 
        likely the same find from which the 
        ANS obtained 14 pieces in 1929 (no. 
        724 above) in which case the heavy 
        preponderance of Byzantine issues 
        suggests Thrace as the more probable 
        findspot
